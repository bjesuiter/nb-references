## Key Principles for App Passive Income

### 1. Choose the Right Business Model

| Model | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-------|------|------|----------|
| **Subscription (SaaS)** | Recurring revenue, predictable | Churn risk, acquisition cost | B2B tools, productivity |
| **Freemium** | Large user base, conversions | Support burden, slow conversion | Consumer apps |
| **One-time Purchase** | Simple, no churn | Single revenue event | Niche utilities |
| **In-App Purchases** | Monetization without paywall | Conversion is hard | Games, social apps |
| **Advertising** | Low friction | Poor UX, low CPM | High-traffic apps |

### 2. Product Selection

**What Works:**
- **Solve your own problem** — You're your first customer
- **Small, specific audience** — Less competition, loyal users
- **Clear value proposition** — One thing, done well
- **Defensible position** — Community, data, network effects

**What Doesn't Work:**
- "Uber for X" — Commodity marketplaces
- Generic utilities — No differentiation
- Trend-chasing — By the time you launch, market moved on

### 3. Build Efficiently

#### Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
- Launch in weeks, not months
- Core feature only — validate before expanding
- Use existing tools (no over-engineering)

#### Tech Stack Considerations
- **Web apps:** Next.js, Remix, SvelteKit
- **Mobile:** React Native, Flutter, SwiftUI
- **Backend:** Supabase, Firebase, Node.js
- **Payments:** Stripe, Lemon Squeezy, Paddle

### 4. Distribution > Product

**Cold Hard Truth:**
> "The best product loses to the best distribution."

**Distribution Channels:**
1. **SEO / Content** — Blog posts, documentation
2. **Product Hunt** — Launch day boost
3. **Twitter/X** — Build in public, share journey
4. **Email list** — Own your audience
5. **Communities** — Reddit, Hacker News, Discord
6. **Partnerships** — Integrations, bundling

### 5. Reduce Churn & Increase LTV

**Churn Prevention:**
- **Onboarding** — First 5 minutes matter most
- **Value delivery** — Show results quickly
- **Engagement** — Notifications, emails, updates
- **Support** — Respond fast, fix bugs faster

**Increase LTV:**
- **Upsells** — Premium features, add-ons
- **Annual plans** — Discount for commitment
- **Referral programs** — Users bring users

### 6. Automate Operations

**What to Automate:**
- **Customer support** — FAQ bots, help docs
- **Billing** — Failed payments, receipts
- **Analytics** — Dashboards, alerts
- **Marketing** — Email sequences, social posts

**Tools:**
- **Support:** Intercom, Crisp, Tidio
- **Email:** ConvertKit, Mailchimp, Resend
- **Analytics:** Plausible, PostHog, Amplitude
- **Payments:** Stripe revenue recovery

### 7. Time Management for Indie Devs

**The 80/20 Rule:**
- 20% of features drive 80% of value
- Focus on what users actually use
- Cut features that don't convert

**Protect Your Time:**
- **No meetings** — Async communication
- **Batch work** — Deep work blocks
- **Outsource** — What you're bad at
- **Automate** — Repetitive tasks

## Steps to Generate Passive Income

### Phase 1: Validation (1-2 weeks)
- [ ] Identify your problem to solve
- [ ] Talk to 5 potential users
- [ ] Create landing page with signup
- [ ] Measure interest (signups, waitlist)

### Phase 2: Build MVP (2-4 weeks)
- [ ] Build core feature only
- [ ] Launch to early adopters
- [ ] Collect feedback rapidly
- [ ] Iterate based on data

### Phase 3: Launch (1-2 weeks)
- [ ] Product Hunt launch prep
- [ ] Email launch to list
- [ ] Share on social (build in public)
- [ ] Community posts (Reddit, HN)

### Phase 4: Optimize (Ongoing)
- [ ] A/B test pricing
- [ ] Improve conversion rates
- [ ] Reduce support burden
- [ ] Scale what works

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target (Year 1) |
|--------|-----------------|
| **MRR** | $1,000-$5,000 |
| **Churn** | < 5% monthly |
| **CAC** | < 3 months LTV |
| **LTV:CAC** | > 3:1 |
| **Conversion** | 2-5% free to paid |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Building in stealth** — Build in public, get early feedback
2. **Perfectionism** — Ship ugly, iterate fast
3. **Ignoring distribution** — Product is only half the battle
4. **Pricing too low** — Leave money on the table
5. **No email list** — Own your audience
