## Reference

### Summary
A communication trick to neutralize manipulative behavior: point out the sneaky/unconscious tactic the other party is using. Making the unconscious conscious disables its power.

### The Trick
When someone uses a sneaky or manipulative communication tactic:
- Simply identify and point out what they're doing
- Making their unconscious tactic conscious/visible neutralizes its effect
- They can no longer use it against you without being openly adversarial

### Why It Works
- Manipulative tactics rely on being unnoticed/unspoken
- Once named, the tactic loses its covert power
- Forces the other party to either stop or openly acknowledge their approach

### Use Cases
- Dealing with passive-aggressive communication
- Handling gaslighting attempts
- neutralizing covert manipulation in conversations
- AI agent prompt injection defense (making the hidden intent visible)
