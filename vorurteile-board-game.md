## Overview

**Vorurteile** (Prejudices) is a German party board game similar to Cards Against Humanity, combining strategic board movement with controversial "prejudice cards."

## Gameplay

### Setup
- Each player creates a character by drawing a **Nationality** (secret) and **Ideology** (open)
- Place your piece on the world map based on a dice roll
- Most "disadvantaged" player starts

### Turn Structure
1. **Draw Phase:** Draw 1 Action card + 1 Prejudice card
2. **Play Phase:** Move your piece (free), play any number of cards, activate ideology ability
3. Pass to next player clockwise

### Winning
- Collect **6 Victory Points**
- Your piece must be on your secret nationality's capital
- Reveal secret nationality to win

### Scoring
- City entries (visited capitals in the city list)
- Played prejudice cards placed on nation-specific board spaces

## Card Types

### Action Cards
- Move your or another player's piece
- Place walls on the board to block paths

## Global Effects

End of each round: draw a Global Effect card
- Triggers after certain prejudice cards (bomb, burning rainforest, etc.)
- Effects: drought, lockdown, flood, even world war
- Most have negative consequences for all players
- Example: Floods block all coastal cities until next global effect

## Similar Games

- **Cards Against Humanity** - Pure card game, no board
- **Vorurteile** - Adds strategic board movement + global consequences
